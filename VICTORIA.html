About the Victoria Terminus

The Victoria Terminus, now known as Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT), is a historic railway station located in the heart of Mumbai, Maharashtra. Built in 1887 to commemorate the Golden Jubilee of Queen Victoria, it is an architectural masterpiece that combines Victorian Gothic and Indian styles. Designed by the British architect Frederick William Stevens, the station is known for its stunning façade, intricate carvings, and grand dome, making it a symbol of Mumbai's colonial past and architectural brilliance. The station's design also incorporates traditional Indian elements like the Basilica-style arches and ornate woodwork. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2004, Victoria Terminus serves as a major railway hub, connecting various parts of Mumbai and the surrounding regions. It handles both local and long-distance trains and is one of the busiest stations in the country. The station's impressive structure, with its blend of Western and Indian elements, reflects the city’s rich history, its colonial heritage, and its vibrant present as a major transport and cultural center. The station also holds significant cultural importance and is a key landmark in Mumbai, attracting tourists and locals alike. The intricate stained glass windows, towers, and statues of great historical figures further enhance its grandeur.
