About HUMAYUN'S TOMB

HUMAYUN'S TOMB, located in DELHI, India, is often referred to as the FIRST GARDEN-TOMB IN THE INDIAN SUBCONTINENT. Built in 1565 by EMPRESS HAMI DA BANU BEGUM, the wife of Emperor HUMAYUN, it marked the beginning of MUGHAL ARCHITECTURE in India and laid the blueprint for the TAJ MAHAL. The tomb is surrounded by a CHARBAGH GARDEN, which is divided into FOUR PERFECTLY SYMMETRICAL QUARTERS, symbolizing PARADISE as described in the QURAN. The symmetrical design represents a harmonious balance between nature and structure. Another fascinating detail is the use of RED SANDSTONE and WHITE MARBLE, creating a striking contrast that gives the tomb its unique elegance. The CENTRAL DOME, standing at 42.5 METERS HIGH, dominates the structure and is a hallmark of Mughal architectural grandeur. Here’s the real kicker: HUMAYUN'S TOMB houses not just the emperor’s remains but also over 150 MUGHAL FAMILY MEMBERS, earning it the nickname “DORMITORY OF THE MUGHALS.”It’s said to be one of the most significant burial sites in India! Lastly, HUMAYUN’S TOMB was declared a UNESCO WORLD HERITAGE SITE in 1993, recognizing its historical and architectural significance. It remains a MASTERPIECE OF MUGHAL DESIGN, blending Persian, Turkish, and Indian styles!
