About the Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, or the "Palace of Breezes," is an architectural marvel that graces the heart of Jaipur, Rajasthan. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this iconic five-story palace was designed by Lal Chand Ustad in the shape of Lord Krishna’s crown, reflecting the king's devotion. The most striking feature of Hawa Mahal is its 953 intricately carved windows, or jharokhas, that allow cool breezes to flow through, making it a perfect retreat during the scorching summers. Made of red and pink sandstone, the palace glows beautifully under the sunlight, creating a mesmerizing sight. Its location at the edge of the City Palace complex offers a commanding view of the bustling streets of the old city, adding to its charm. The honeycomb-like structure is a brilliant fusion of Rajput and Mughal architecture, combining vibrant artistry with functional design. Exploring its narrow passages, spiral staircases, and panoramic views from the top offers visitors a chance to experience the grandeur of royal Rajasthan. Today, it is one of India’s most photographed landmarks and a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts, architecture lovers, and anyone captivated by the magic of Rajasthan.
