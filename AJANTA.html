About AJANTA ELLORA

The AJANTA CAVES and ELLORA CAVES, located in MAHARASHTRA, India, are UNESCO WORLD HERITAGE SITES and WONDERS OF ANCIENT INDIAN ART AND ARCHITECTURE. What sets these two apart is their UNIQUE COMBINATION OF RELIGIOUS, ARTISTIC, AND ENGINEERING EXCELLENCE. The AJANTA CAVES, carved between the 2ND CENTURY BCE and the 6TH CENTURY CE, are a series of 29 ROCK-CUT BUDDHIST CAVES. They are renowned for their STUNNING PAINTINGS AND FRESCOES, which depict scenes from the LIFE OF BUDDHA and JATAKA TALES. The paintings, created using natural dyes, are so vibrant and detailed that they have survived over 2,000 YEARS, showcasing the ARTISTIC GENIUS OF ANCIENT INDIA. The ELLORA CAVES, on the other hand, feature a mix of BUDDHIST, HINDU, AND JAIN MONUMENTS, carved between the 6TH AND 10TH CENTURIES CE. The highlight of Ellora is the KAILASA TEMPLE (CAVE 16), which is the LARGEST MONOLITHIC ROCK-CUT STRUCTURE IN THE WORLD. It was carved from a single rock and mimics the MOUNT KAILASH, the abode of Lord Shiva. This architectural feat was achieved by removing over 200,000 TONS OF ROCK, and the precision with which it was built continues to amaze modern engineers.
