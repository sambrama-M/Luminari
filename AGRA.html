About the Agra Fort

The Agra Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a magnificent Mughal fortress located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh. Built primarily by Emperor Akbar in the 16th century, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until the capital was shifted to Delhi. The fort is renowned for its impressive architecture, blending Persian, Timurid, and Indian styles. The fort's massive walls, made of red sandstone, stand 21 meters high and stretch over 2.5 kilometers, enclosing a series of stunning palaces, mosques, and gardens. Some of its most notable structures include the Jahangir Mahal, Diwan-i-Am, Diwan-i-Khas, and the Moti Masjid. The fort also houses the Khas Mahal, a beautiful marble palace, and the Sheesh Mahal, a palace of mirrors. The Shah Jahan Mahal, with its delicate marble work, adds to the grandeur of the fort. The fort played a significant role in India's history, including being the place where Shah Jahan was imprisoned by his son Aurangzeb, and it offers breathtaking views of the nearby Taj Mahal. The Nagina Masjid, Musamman Burj, and the Khaas Mahal are also important features, with the latter being the location where Shah Jahan spent his final years in captivity, gazing at the Taj Mahal. Agra Fort also houses several gates, including the grand Delhi Gate and the Amar Singh Gate, which offer historical significance and are architectural marvels in their own right. As a symbol of Mughal power and architectural brilliance, Agra Fort is not only a historical monument but also a key cultural landmark, attracting millions of tourists annually.
