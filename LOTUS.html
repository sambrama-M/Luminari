About the Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, located in the heart of New Delhi, India, is a striking architectural masterpiece and a symbol of peace and unity. Shaped like a blooming lotus flower, this Bahai House of Worship is open to people of all faiths, inviting them to come together in silent meditation and prayer. With its pristine white marble petals rising gracefully from a reflecting pool, the temple blends modern design with spiritual symbolism. The Lotus Temple's design, with its intricate symmetry and natural beauty, is not only an architectural wonder but also a powerful reminder of the Bahai teachings of unity, harmony, and the oneness of humanity. The temple's unique structure, consisting of 27 freestanding marble-clad petals arranged in clusters, creates a sense of openness and inclusivity, allowing light to flow into the prayer hall. The temple is also known for its eco-friendly features, such as its rainwater harvesting system, adding an environmental touch to its spiritual purpose. Open to all visitors, regardless of religion or background, the Lotus Temple encourages reflection on the shared values of love, peace, and respect for all. Its timeless beauty and message of unity continue to inspire millions around the world.
