About the Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is located in Amritsar, Punjab, and is one of the most revered religious sites in the world. It is the holiest shrine of the Sikh religion, attracting millions of devotees and visitors annually. The temple's striking architecture features a gold-plated structure, which gives it its name, and sits serenely in the middle of a vast, sacred pool known as the Amrit Sarovar. The Golden Temple was founded in the late 16th century by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru, and later expanded by Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Guru, who compiled the Adi Granth, the central religious scripture of Sikhism, which is housed in the temple. The temple's design combines elements of Islamic, Hindu, and Sikh architecture, symbolizing unity and inclusivity. Visitors can partake in the practice of Langar, a community kitchen that serves free meals to all, regardless of background. The Golden Temple stands not only as a spiritual beacon but also as a symbol of peace, humility, and selfless service.

The Golden Temple is a symbol of Sikh values such as equality, humility, and selfless service. Its serene atmosphere and stunning architecture make it a profound spiritual and cultural landmark, fostering a sense of peace and unity among people from all walks of life.
